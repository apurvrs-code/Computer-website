Computer Chapter 6 - Cyber Ethics

Introduction Importance of Cyber Ethics Common Cyber Crimes Cyber Security Rules Dos and Don'ts

Cyber Ethics refers to the code of responsible behavior while using computers and the Internet.

It includes respecting others' privacy, data, and intellectual property while using digital resources.

  • Maintains online safety and security.
  • Prevents misuse of technology and digital resources.
  • Protects personal information and privacy.
  • Encourages responsible and ethical online behavior.
  • Hacking – unauthorized access to computers and networks.
  • Phishing – stealing sensitive information through fake websites/emails.
  • Cyberbullying – harassing or threatening people online.
  • Identity Theft – stealing personal information for fraud.
  • Spamming – sending unwanted emails/messages repeatedly.
  • Use strong and unique passwords for accounts.
  • Install antivirus and update regularly.
  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links or attachments.
  • Secure personal devices with passwords or biometrics.
  • Regularly back up important data.
  • Do: Respect others' privacy, report suspicious activities, stay updated on security tips.
  • Don't: Share personal passwords, post harmful content, download pirated software, hack systems.
